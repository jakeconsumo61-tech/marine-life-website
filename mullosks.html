Mollusk

Soft bodies hidden behind armor or vanished into ink. From the giant squid’s eye to the snail’s spiral shell, they are the masters of adaptation and intelligence. Meet Mollusca—an ancient lineage of builders, hunters, and masters of disguise.

Mollusk

Clown Nudibranch

A striking sea slug with a translucent white body covered in bright orange or yellow "papillae." They are common in the chilly tide pools of the Pacific Northwest.

Fun Fact

They feed almost exclusively on bryozoans (tiny moss animals) and are actually able to swim by flexing their bodies back and forth in a frantic wiggle.

ScientificTriopha catalinae
HabitatEastern Pacific
SizeUp to 7 cm
DietBryozoans
Mollusk

Ringed Chromodoris

Easily recognized by its creamy white body and distinct purple circles or rings. These slugs move slowly across the reef, advertising their toxicity with bright colors.

Fun Fact

They are "chemical thieves"—they eat toxic sponges and concentrate the poison in their own skin to make themselves taste terrible to fish.

ScientificGoniobranchus annulatus
HabitatIndian Ocean & Red Sea
SizeUp to 6 cm
DietSponges
Mollusk

Elisabeth's Chromodoris

A stunning nudibranch with electric blue, black, and white longitudinal stripes and bright orange gills that look like a small bouquet on its back.

Fun Fact

When they feel vibrations in the water, they can instantly retract their feathery gills into a specialized pocket for protection.

ScientificChromodoris elisabethina
HabitatWestern Pacific
SizeUp to 5 cm
DietSponges
Mollusk

Sap-sucking Slug

Looking like a tiny green leaf or a "sea sheep," these slugs are famous for their ability to incorporate algae into their own biology.

Fun Fact

They are "solar-powered"! They steal chloroplasts from the algae they eat and use them to perform photosynthesis inside their own bodies for energy.

ScientificCostasiella kuroshimae
HabitatTropical Reefs
SizeUp to 1 cm
DietAlgae Sap
Mollusk

Varied Headshield Slug

These slugs have a flattened, shield-like head used for plowing through sand and silt. They are often dark purple or black with neon blue or orange edges.

Fun Fact

They are actually hunters! They follow the slime trails of other sea slugs and worms to track them down and eat them.

ScientificChelidonura varians
HabitatIndo-Pacific
SizeUp to 7 cm
DietFlatworms
Mollusk

Lined Thuridilla

A colorful sacoglossan slug with long, elegant lines of yellow, blue, and black running the length of its velvety green body.

Fun Fact

They use their "rhinophores" (the two ear-like stalks on their head) to literally "smell" the water for their favorite type of green seaweed.

ScientificThuridilla lineolata
HabitatIndo-Pacific
SizeUp to 3 cm
DietAlgae
Mollusk

Cratena Nudibranch

A delicate-looking slug with "cerata" (finger-like growths) on its back that can range from fiery orange to deep blue depending on what it has recently eaten.

Fun Fact

They eat hydroids (relatives of jellyfish) and can store the hydroid's stinging cells in their own back to shoot at any fish that tries to bite them!

ScientificCratena peregrina
HabitatMediterranean & Atlantic
SizeUp to 5 cm
DietHydroids
Mollusk

Variable Neon Slug

One of the brightest nudibranchs, often appearing in glowing neon green or blue with black and orange highlights. They are common on tropical reefs.

Fun Fact

Their bright colors are a warning called "aposematism," telling predators: "Don't eat me, I'm packed with unpleasant toxins!"

ScientificNembrotha kubaryana
HabitatIndo-Pacific
SizeUp to 12 cm
DietAscidians (Sea Squirts)
Mollusk

Tryon's Risbecia

An elegant slug with a pale white or lavender body speckled with small, dark purple dots. They have a high, thin profile as they crawl.

Fun Fact

They are famous for "trailing"—one slug will follow directly behind another, touching its tail, forming a tiny nudibranch train across the sand.

ScientificHypselodoris tryoni
HabitatTropical West Pacific
SizeUp to 7 cm
DietSponges
Mollusk

Opalescent Nudibranch

A "shag rug" sea slug from the Pacific coast. It has a pearly body and hundreds of neon-blue-tipped cerata along its back.

Fun Fact

They are extremely aggressive for their size and will willingly attack and eat other nudibranchs that cross their path.

ScientificHermissenda crassicornis
HabitatNorth American Pacific
SizeUp to 8 cm
DietAnemones & Slugs
Mollusk

Spanish Shawl

With a purple body, orange cerata, and scarlet rhinophores, this nudibranch is named after the brightly colored shawls worn by Spanish dancers.

Fun Fact

Unlike most slugs that only crawl, the Spanish Shawl can swim through the water column by whipping its body into a "U" shape to escape predators.

ScientificFlabellinopsis iodinea
HabitatCalifornia Coast
SizeUp to 7 cm
DietHydroids
Cephalopod

Common Octopus

The most intelligent of all mollusks, the Common Octopus is a master of camouflage, able to change both its color and skin texture in milliseconds.

Fun Fact

They have three hearts and blue blood! Two hearts pump blood to the gills, while the third pumps it to the rest of the body.

ScientificOctopus vulgaris
HabitatGlobal Coastal Waters
SizeUp to 90 cm
DietCrabs & Mollusks
Cephalopod

Common Cuttlefish

Known as the "chameleons of the sea," cuttlefish use pulsating waves of color to communicate and hunt. They have a unique internal shell called a cuttlebone.

Fun Fact

They have W-shaped pupils that allow them to see polarized light, helping them spot camouflaged prey that other animals would miss.

ScientificSepia officinalis
HabitatEast Atlantic & Med
SizeUp to 45 cm
DietSmall Fish & Crabs
Marine Fish

Red Sea Bream

A highly prized fish in many cultures, the Red Sea Bream is known for its beautiful pinkish-red hue and its importance in the coastal ecosystems of Japan and Europe.

Fun Fact

In Japan, this fish is known as "Tai" and is a symbol of good luck and celebration, often served at weddings.

ScientificPagrus major
HabitatNorthwest Pacific
SizeUp to 1.2 meters
DietCrabs & Small Fish
Marine Fish

Ribbon Eel (Yellow)

This is the final life stage of the Ribbon Eel. In the yellow phase, the eel has reached full maturity and is a functional female, living in sand or rubble holes.

Fun Fact

They are protandrous hermaphrodites, meaning they all start as black juveniles, turn blue as males, and finally yellow as females!

ScientificRhinomuraena quaesita
HabitatIndo-Pacific
SizeUp to 1 meter
DietSmall Fish
Marine Fish

Nassau Grouper

A social and curious fish, the Nassau Grouper is a top predator of Caribbean reefs. They have a distinctive "tuning fork" pattern on their forehead.

Fun Fact

They are known to be very "friendly" to divers and often follow them around the reef like underwater puppies.

ScientificEpinephelus striatus
HabitatCaribbean & Atlantic
SizeUp to 1 meter
DietCrabs & Spiny Lobsters
Marine Fish

Clownfish

Also known as Anemonefish, these bright orange fish are famous for their symbiotic relationship with stinging sea anemones. They are one of the few species capable of living safely among the anemone's venomous tentacles.

Fun Fact

They aren't born immune to the sting! They perform a special "dance" to coat their bodies in the anemone's mucus, tricking the anemone into thinking the fish is part of itself.

ScientificAmphiprioninae
HabitatIndo-Pacific Reefs
Size7 to 10 cm
DietAlgae & Zooplankton
Marine Fish

Blue Tang

Famous for its vibrant royal blue body and yellow tail, the Blue Tang is a critical member of the coral reef. They are tireless grazers that help keep coral heads clean from overgrowing algae.

Fun Fact

They are "surgeons" of the sea! They have a small, retractable scalpel-like spine on each side of their tail base for defense against predators.

ScientificParacanthurus hepatus
HabitatIndo-Pacific Reefs
SizeUp to 30 cm
DietAlgae & Plankton
Freshwater Fish

Corydoras Catfish

Often called "Corys," these small, armored catfish are the vacuum cleaners of South American rivers. They are social bottom-dwellers that use their sensitive barbels to find food in the substrate.

Fun Fact

They are "intestinal breathers"—they can dash to the surface, gulp air, and absorb the oxygen through their gut to survive in low-oxygen waters.

ScientificCorydoras spp.
HabitatAmazon River Basin
Size2 to 10 cm
DietBenthic Detritus & Insects
Marine Fish

Blue Ring Angelfish

Named for the sapphire-blue ring located just behind its head, this majestic fish is a staple of Indo-Pacific coral gardens. They are known for their graceful swimming and striking facial markings.

Fun Fact

They are surprisingly vocal! When they feel threatened or territorial, they can produce loud, audible "drumming" sounds to scare away intruders.

ScientificPomacanthus annuarlis
HabitatTropical West Pacific
SizeUp to 45 cm
DietSponges & Tunicates
Marine Fish

Black Sea Bass

A robust, bottom-dwelling fish commonly found around shipwrecks and jetties. They are highly sought after by anglers for their fighting spirit and mottled charcoal-to-black coloration.

Fun Fact

They are protogynous hermaphrodites, meaning most start life as females and later transform into dominant males once they reach a certain age or size.

ScientificCentropristis striata
HabitatWestern Atlantic Coast
SizeUp to 60 cm
DietCrabs, Mussels & Small Fish
