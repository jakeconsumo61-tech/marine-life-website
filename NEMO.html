Bony Fish

Architects of the coral reef and masters of the open blue. From shimmering schools to the deep-sea giants, they breathe through gills and navigate with skeletons of bone. Meet the Osteichthyes—the world’s most diverse vertebrates, ruling every corner of the liquid world.

Marine Fish

Porcupine Puffer

Commonly known as Burrfish, these puffers are covered in sharp spines that usually lay flat against the body. When threatened, they swallow water to inflate into a prickly ball that is impossible to swallow.

Fun Fact

Unlike regular pufferfish, Porcupinefish have heavy, beak-like fused teeth that allow them to crush the hardest shells of sea urchins and crabs.

ScientificDiodon holocanthus
HabitatGlobal Tropical Waters
SizeUp to 50 cm
DietCrustaceans & Urchins
Marine Fish

Longhorn Cowfish

Recognizable by the long "horns" protruding from its head and rear, this boxfish moves with a unique hovering motion. They are bright yellow or tan with white spots and have a rigid, armor-like body.

Fun Fact

When stressed or threatened, they can release a potent toxin called ostracitoxin through their skin, which can be lethal to other fish in the vicinity.

ScientificLactoria cornuta
HabitatIndo-Pacific Reefs
SizeUp to 45 cm
DietBenthic Algae & Worms
Marine Fish

Sarcastic Fringehead

Small but incredibly aggressive, the Fringehead is famous for its territorial behavior. They live in shells or holes and will snap at anything that gets too close.

Fun Fact

When two males fight, they open their massive, colorful mouths and press them together, as if "kissing," to see who is larger.

ScientificNeoclinus blanchardi
HabitatPacific (California)
SizeUp to 30 cm
DietSquid & Fish Eggs
Marine Fish

Malabar Grouper

A large, robust predator found in lagoons and seaward reefs. They are master camouflagers, using their mottled brown skin to blend into muddy or rocky bottoms.

Fun Fact

Like many groupers, they can change sex as they grow, usually starting as females and becoming males when they reach a certain size.

ScientificEpinephelus malabaricus
HabitatIndo-Pacific
SizeUp to 2.3 meters
DietFish & Crustaceans
Marine Fish

Valentini Puffer

A charming, small pufferfish with black saddles and neon spots. They are slow swimmers but highly protected by their toxic skin and ability to puff up.

Fun Fact

The filefish *Paraluteres prionurus* has evolved to look exactly like this puffer to trick predators into thinking it is toxic!

ScientificCanthigaster valentini
HabitatTropical Reefs
SizeUp to 11 cm
DietAlgae & Invertebrates
Marine Fish

Oriental Sweetlips

Named for their unusually large, fleshy lips, these fish are stunning as adults with horizontal black and yellow stripes. They are nocturnal hunters that rest during the day.

Fun Fact

Juvenile Sweetlips swim with a weird, wiggly motion that makes them look like toxic flatworms to avoid being eaten.

ScientificPlectorhinchus vittatus
HabitatIndian Ocean & Pacific
SizeUp to 86 cm
DietBenthic Invertebrates
Marine Fish

Marine Betta (Comet)

A shy, nocturnal fish with a dark body covered in white spots. They are masters of mimicry and use their large fins to confuse both prey and predators.

Fun Fact

When threatened, it pokes its tail out of a hole. The tail has a fake eye spot, making it look like the head of a Moray Eel!

ScientificCalloplesiops altivelis
HabitatIndo-Pacific Reefs
SizeUp to 20 cm
DietSmall Fish & Shrimp
Marine Fish

Pajama Cardinalfish

With a yellow face, a dark vertical band, and polka-dot rear fins, this fish looks like it's wearing mismatched pajamas. They are peaceful fish that school together.

Fun Fact

They are "mouthbrooders," meaning the male carries the eggs in his mouth to protect them until they hatch.

ScientificSphaeramia nematoptera
HabitatWestern Pacific
SizeUp to 8 cm
DietSmall Crustaceans
Marine Fish

Painted Frogfish

A master of disguise, the Painted Frogfish can change its color to match its surroundings over several weeks. They don't swim much, preferring to "walk" on their fins.

Fun Fact

They have a built-in fishing rod on their head (an illicium) with a lure that they wiggle to attract unsuspecting fish.

ScientificAntennarius pictus
HabitatIndo-Pacific
SizeUp to 30 cm
DietSmall Fish
Marine Fish

Panther Grouper

Easily identified by its white body and large black spots, the Panther Grouper has a unique silhouette with a slender head and a high, hunched back.

Fun Fact

Because of its elegant swimming and striking spots, it is often called the "Graceful Grouper" or "Barramundi Cod."

ScientificCromileptes altivelis
HabitatWestern Pacific
SizeUp to 70 cm
DietFish & Small Nekton
Marine Fish

Naso Tang

Also known as the Orangespine Unicornfish, these beautiful surgeons feature bright orange spines at the base of their tail and a distinctive "lipstick" marking around their mouths.

Fun Fact

The two spines near their tail are as sharp as scalpels and are used as defensive weapons against predators.

ScientificNaso lituratus
HabitatIndo-Pacific
SizeUp to 45 cm
DietBrown Macroalgae
Marine Fish

Narrow-lined Puffer

This puffer is covered in thin, dark parallel lines that run across its body. Like all puffers, it contains tetrodotoxin, making it highly lethal if eaten.

Fun Fact

They use their beak-like teeth to crunch through the hardest shells, including coral and crabs.

ScientificArothron manilensis
HabitatWestern Pacific
SizeUp to 31 cm
DietMollusks & Algae
Marine Fish

Ocean Sunfish (Mola)

The Mola mola is one of the heaviest bony fish in the world. They look like a giant floating head with wings, often found basking on their sides at the surface.

Fun Fact

A single female can produce up to 300 million eggs at a time, more than any other known vertebrate!

ScientificMola mola
HabitatGlobal Temperate Waters
SizeUp to 3.3 meters
DietJellyfish & Zooplankton
Marine Fish

Red Sea Bream

A highly prized fish in many cultures, the Red Sea Bream is known for its beautiful pinkish-red hue and its importance in the coastal ecosystems of Japan and Europe.

Fun Fact

In Japan, this fish is known as "Tai" and is a symbol of good luck and celebration, often served at weddings.

ScientificPagrus major
HabitatNorthwest Pacific
SizeUp to 1.2 meters
DietCrabs & Small Fish
Marine Fish

Ribbon Eel (Yellow)

This is the final life stage of the Ribbon Eel. In the yellow phase, the eel has reached full maturity and is a functional female, living in sand or rubble holes.

Fun Fact

They are protandrous hermaphrodites, meaning they all start as black juveniles, turn blue as males, and finally yellow as females!

ScientificRhinomuraena quaesita
HabitatIndo-Pacific
SizeUp to 1 meter
DietSmall Fish
Marine Fish

Nassau Grouper

A social and curious fish, the Nassau Grouper is a top predator of Caribbean reefs. They have a distinctive "tuning fork" pattern on their forehead.

Fun Fact

They are known to be very "friendly" to divers and often follow them around the reef like underwater puppies.

ScientificEpinephelus striatus
HabitatCaribbean & Atlantic
SizeUp to 1 meter
DietCrabs & Spiny Lobsters
Marine Fish

Clownfish

Also known as Anemonefish, these bright orange fish are famous for their symbiotic relationship with stinging sea anemones. They are one of the few species capable of living safely among the anemone's venomous tentacles.

Fun Fact

They aren't born immune to the sting! They perform a special "dance" to coat their bodies in the anemone's mucus, tricking the anemone into thinking the fish is part of itself.

ScientificAmphiprioninae
HabitatIndo-Pacific Reefs
Size7 to 10 cm
DietAlgae & Zooplankton
Marine Fish

Blue Tang

Famous for its vibrant royal blue body and yellow tail, the Blue Tang is a critical member of the coral reef. They are tireless grazers that help keep coral heads clean from overgrowing algae.

Fun Fact

They are "surgeons" of the sea! They have a small, retractable scalpel-like spine on each side of their tail base for defense against predators.

ScientificParacanthurus hepatus
HabitatIndo-Pacific Reefs
SizeUp to 30 cm
DietAlgae & Plankton
Freshwater Fish

Corydoras Catfish

Often called "Corys," these small, armored catfish are the vacuum cleaners of South American rivers. They are social bottom-dwellers that use their sensitive barbels to find food in the substrate.

Fun Fact

They are "intestinal breathers"—they can dash to the surface, gulp air, and absorb the oxygen through their gut to survive in low-oxygen waters.

ScientificCorydoras spp.
HabitatAmazon River Basin
Size2 to 10 cm
DietBenthic Detritus & Insects
Marine Fish

Blue Ring Angelfish

Named for the sapphire-blue ring located just behind its head, this majestic fish is a staple of Indo-Pacific coral gardens. They are known for their graceful swimming and striking facial markings.

Fun Fact

They are surprisingly vocal! When they feel threatened or territorial, they can produce loud, audible "drumming" sounds to scare away intruders.

ScientificPomacanthus annuarlis
HabitatTropical West Pacific
SizeUp to 45 cm
DietSponges & Tunicates
Marine Fish

Black Sea Bass

A robust, bottom-dwelling fish commonly found around shipwrecks and jetties. They are highly sought after by anglers for their fighting spirit and mottled charcoal-to-black coloration.

Fun Fact

They are protogynous hermaphrodites, meaning most start life as females and later transform into dominant males once they reach a certain age or size.

ScientificCentropristis striata
HabitatWestern Atlantic Coast
SizeUp to 60 cm
DietCrabs, Mussels & Small Fish
