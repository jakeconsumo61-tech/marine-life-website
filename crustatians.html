Crustaceans

The armored knights of the ocean floor. Armed with heavy claws and segmented suits of mail, they scavenge, hunt, and thrive in every crevice of the sea. Meet the Crustacea—ten-legged wonders that keep the world’s oceans clean and its food webs strong.

Crustacean

American Lobster

The heavyweight champion of the Atlantic, known for its massive claws. One claw is a blunt "crusher" for shells, while the other is a sharp "seizer" for soft prey.

Fun Fact

Lobsters are biologically immortal in a way; they don't weaken with age, but rather grow larger and more fertile until they physically can't molt anymore.

ScientificHomarus americanus
HabitatNorth Atlantic Coast
SizeUp to 60 cm
DietFish & Mollusks
Crustacean

Caribbean Spiny Lobster

Unlike their Atlantic cousins, these lobsters lack large claws. Instead, they are covered in sharp forward-pointing spines and possess incredibly long, sensitive antennae.

Fun Fact

They are famous for their "marches"—hundreds of lobsters form a single-file line on the ocean floor to migrate to deeper water during storms.

ScientificPanulirus argus
HabitatTropical Atlantic
SizeUp to 60 cm
DietSnails & Crabs
Crustacean

Peppermint Shrimp

Named for its red and white candy-cane stripes, this shrimp is a favorite in home aquariums. They are nocturnal cleaners that scan the reef for parasites and debris.

Fun Fact

They are highly valued by reef keepers because they are one of the few creatures that eat the pest anemone, Aiptasia.

ScientificLysmata wurdemanni
HabitatWest Atlantic Reefs
SizeUp to 5 cm
DietParasites & Detritus
Crustacean

Painted Spiny Lobster

One of the most colorful lobsters, featuring blue-green shells with white spots and intricate patterns. They prefer shallow reefs and rocky crevices in the Indo-Pacific.

Fun Fact

They make a loud rasping sound by rubbing their antennae bases against their heads to startle predators—like a violin string being plucked.

ScientificPanulirus versicolor
HabitatIndo-Pacific
SizeUp to 40 cm
DietCarrion & Small Mollusks
Crustacean

Halloween Crab

Also called the Moon Crab, this land-dwelling species is unforgettable with its bright orange legs, purple claws, and deep black carapace.

Fun Fact

Despite living in the forest, they must return to the ocean to release their eggs, which hatch into marine larvae before returning to land.

ScientificGecarcinus quadratus
HabitatCentral American Forests
SizeUp to 5 cm (Shell)
DietLeaves & Seedlings
Crustacean

Green Shore Crab

A highly adaptable and resilient crab found in rocky intertidal zones. Though "green" is in the name, their color can range from yellow to dark red.

Fun Fact

They are incredibly tough; they can survive for several days out of water by keeping their gills moist in damp crevices.

ScientificCarcinus maenas
HabitatEuropean & North Atlantic
SizeUp to 9 cm (Shell)
DietWorms & Bivalves
Crustacean

Signal Crayfish

Native to North America, this crayfish is easily identified by the white "signal" patch at the joint of its claws. They are aggressive and highly successful freshwater explorers.

Fun Fact

They are a major invasive species in Europe, where they carry a "crayfish plague" that is harmless to them but deadly to native European crayfish.

ScientificPacifastacus leniusculus
HabitatRivers & Streams
SizeUp to 15 cm
DietInsects & Detritus
Crustacean

European Crayfish

Also known as the Noble Crayfish, this is the most common native crayfish in Europe. They are brown or green-blue and are considered a bio-indicator of clean water.

Fun Fact

In many Scandinavian cultures, the "Crayfish Party" is a major summer tradition centered around eating this specific species.

ScientificAstacus astacus
HabitatFreshwater Lakes
SizeUp to 16 cm
DietPlants & Invertebrates
Crustacean

Durban Dancing Shrimp

A small, ornate shrimp with red and white patterns. They get their name from their constant, rhythmic rocking motion while they stand on the reef.

Fun Fact

Their "dancing" isn't just for show; it's thought to help break up their silhouette, making it harder for predators to track their exact shape.

ScientificRhynchocinetes durbanensis
HabitatIndo-Pacific Reefs
SizeUp to 4 cm
DietPlankton & Detritus
Crustacean

Sally Lightfoot Crab

Brilliantly multicolored and incredibly fast, these crabs are the acrobats of the Galapagos rocky shores. They can run in four directions and even "skate" across water.

Fun Fact

They have a unique relationship with Marine Iguanas, acting as "cleaners" by eating ticks and parasites off the iguanas' skin.

ScientificGrapsus grapsus
HabitatAmericas Coastal Rocks
Size8 to 12 cm
DietAlgae & Small Bits
Crustacean

Emerald Crab

A small, bright green crab with a bumpy shell and hairy legs. They are the guardians of the reef, spending their lives tucked into small holes in the rockwork.

Fun Fact

They are the only crabs widely known to eat Bubble Algae (Valonia), which is a nuisance that most other reef animals won't touch.

ScientificMithraculus sculptus
HabitatCaribbean Reefs
SizeUp to 4 cm
DietBubble Algae & Meaty Bits
Crustacean

Peacock Mantis Shrimp

A vibrant, rainbow-colored predator with a deadly punch. They are "smashers," using club-like appendages to shatter the shells of snails and crabs with ease.

Fun Fact

Their strike is as fast as a .22 caliber bullet, creating heat and light (sonoluminescence) under the water from the sheer force of the impact.

ScientificOdontodactylus scyllarus
HabitatIndo-Pacific
SizeUp to 18 cm
DietCrabs & Snails
Crustacean

Spotted Porcelain Crab

Despite their name, they aren't "true" crabs. They have flattened bodies and large, delicate-looking claws used mostly for show and territorial disputes.

Fun Fact

They are filter feeders! They use fan-like appendages called maxillipeds to "comb" the water for plankton and tiny food particles.

ScientificNeopetrolisthes maculatus
HabitatAnemone Symbiont
SizeUp to 3 cm
DietPlankton & Mucus
Crustacean

Left-Handed Hermit

Named because their left claw is significantly larger than their right, acting as a "shield" or "door" when they retreat into their borrowed shells.

Fun Fact

When a large new shell is found, hermits often form a "vacancy chain," where they line up by size to swap shells until everyone has a better home.

ScientificCalcinus laevimanus
HabitatIntertidal Reefs
SizeUp to 4 cm
DietAlgae & Detritus
Crustacean

Porcelain Anemone Crab

This variety lives in a symbiotic relationship with large sea anemones. They are "anemone crabs" that enjoy the protection of the stinging tentacles.

Fun Fact

If they lose a limb to a predator, they can "autotomize" (drop) the limb and grow a perfectly new one during their next few molts.

ScientificNeopetrolisthes oshimai
HabitatIndo-Pacific
SizeUp to 2.5 cm
DietSuspended Particles
Crustacean

Harlequin Mantis

Beyond their physical strength, these shrimp have the most complex eyes in the animal kingdom, capable of moving independently and seeing circular polarized light.

Fun Fact

While humans have 3 color receptors (cones) in our eyes, the Mantis Shrimp has 16! They see a world of color we can't even imagine.

ScientificOdontodactylus scyllarus
HabitatCoral Reef Burrows
SizeUp to 18 cm
DietHard-shelled Prey
Marine Fish

Clownfish

Also known as Anemonefish, these bright orange fish are famous for their symbiotic relationship with stinging sea anemones. They are one of the few species capable of living safely among the anemone's venomous tentacles.

Fun Fact

They aren't born immune to the sting! They perform a special "dance" to coat their bodies in the anemone's mucus, tricking the anemone into thinking the fish is part of itself.

ScientificAmphiprioninae
HabitatIndo-Pacific Reefs
Size7 to 10 cm
DietAlgae & Zooplankton
Marine Fish

Blue Tang

Famous for its vibrant royal blue body and yellow tail, the Blue Tang is a critical member of the coral reef. They are tireless grazers that help keep coral heads clean from overgrowing algae.

Fun Fact

They are "surgeons" of the sea! They have a small, retractable scalpel-like spine on each side of their tail base for defense against predators.

ScientificParacanthurus hepatus
HabitatIndo-Pacific Reefs
SizeUp to 30 cm
DietAlgae & Plankton
Freshwater Fish

Corydoras Catfish

Often called "Corys," these small, armored catfish are the vacuum cleaners of South American rivers. They are social bottom-dwellers that use their sensitive barbels to find food in the substrate.

Fun Fact

They are "intestinal breathers"—they can dash to the surface, gulp air, and absorb the oxygen through their gut to survive in low-oxygen waters.

ScientificCorydoras spp.
HabitatAmazon River Basin
Size2 to 10 cm
DietBenthic Detritus & Insects
Marine Fish

Blue Ring Angelfish

Named for the sapphire-blue ring located just behind its head, this majestic fish is a staple of Indo-Pacific coral gardens. They are known for their graceful swimming and striking facial markings.

Fun Fact

They are surprisingly vocal! When they feel threatened or territorial, they can produce loud, audible "drumming" sounds to scare away intruders.

ScientificPomacanthus annuarlis
HabitatTropical West Pacific
SizeUp to 45 cm
DietSponges & Tunicates
Marine Fish

Black Sea Bass

A robust, bottom-dwelling fish commonly found around shipwrecks and jetties. They are highly sought after by anglers for their fighting spirit and mottled charcoal-to-black coloration.

Fun Fact

They are protogynous hermaphrodites, meaning most start life as females and later transform into dominant males once they reach a certain age or size.

ScientificCentropristis striata
HabitatWestern Atlantic Coast
SizeUp to 60 cm
DietCrabs, Mussels & Small Fish
